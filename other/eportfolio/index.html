Reflection Statement


   I think the easiest thing to reflect on for this course is the fact that I am terrible at writing. I barely passed my high school English honors classes, I didn’t write an essay for any of my college applications, and the only reason I even took this course before 1101 is because I aced the multiple-choice part of the CLEP exam. ENC2135 has been just as hard for me as you would expect: at the time of writing this (December 6), there is a chance I will end up failing the course.

   This is partly because I procrastinate way too much. I struggle to get the motivation to start assignments, especially if they are large ones or when I have no interest in the subjects. Everything in this course fits both of those perfectly. Although I did get to pick the topic, I am much more interested in solving technical problems (I am a computer science major) rather than writing or composing media. On all the projects in this course, I started right before or even after the due date, and I suffered because of it.

   I am also not great at the actual writing process. I struggle to reach word counts, because I always feel like I can adequately cover the topic in fewer words. I also don’t usually do drafts, because I usually feel like what I write the first time is the best I am going to get, or the effort to re-write it is not worth the improvement. As a result, it takes my way longer to write fewer words, which in turn makes assignments feel even bigger, and then harder to get motivated to start it. On all of the projects in this course, I submitted at most one draft and I never met the required or recommended word counts.

   Despite this, I do feel that I have learned a lot from this course – more than I have learned in all English throughout high school. Though my coursework may not reflect it, I think I have a better understanding of all the concepts this course was designed to teach. For Project 1, I had to learn how to compose a piece of writing without including any of my own ideas, which was tough for a lot of the students in my class. It was also the first time I had even heard about an annotated bibliography. Project 2 and the Believing and Doubting Game helped me build on critical reading skills a great deal. In addition to simply analyzing the argument and possible biases of the author, as I had learned to do in high school, I started thinking about how the genre conventions, affordances, and restraints, as well as the intended audience and rhetorical strategies related to the message and each other.

   I have to admit, at the time of writing this reflection, I have not started Project 3. I will plan it out tonight and do the whole thing tomorrow morning before my final exams. I’m also supposed to also revise one of Project 1 or 2, but I don’t think I will have time to do that at all.
